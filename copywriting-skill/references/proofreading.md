# Proofreading — the 7 pre-ship scans

Before the quality gates. A factual, logical, or mechanical bug in copy reads as a brand-trust collapse. The reader catches it in half a second and loses faith in the entire draft.

Proofreading is binary. Pass or fail. Not stylistic.

## The 7 scans (run in order)

### Scan 1 — Count vs. list match

Any sentence that names a number must match the list that follows.

**Bug:** "Four words define this brand. Thicker. Fuller. Faster." (Said four. Listed three.)
**Fix:** Change the count OR add the fourth item.

**Procedure:**
- Scan for number words: two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten, dozen, hundred.
- Scan for digit forms: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10.
- For each hit, count the listed items that follow.
- If the count doesn't match, fail.

**Also catches:** "A trio of..." followed by four items. "Both X and Y and Z" ("both" means two). "Three reasons:" followed by two bullets.

### Scan 2 — Antecedent match

Every pronoun (*he, she, it, they, this, that, these, those, his, her, their, its*) points to one visibly named antecedent within the prior two sentences.

**Bug:** "Marco met with Bryan. He said the deck was strong." Who said? Marco or Bryan?
**Fix:** Replace the pronoun with the named subject.

**Also catches:** Orphan "this" — "This is the opportunity" — when "this" has no clear referent in the sentence before.

### Scan 3 — Named-entity & number consistency

Proper nouns spelled the same every time. Numbers agree with earlier references.

**Common bugs:**
- Person's name misspelled across paragraphs ("Marko" once, "Marco" later).
- Brand-name variants ("The Beard Club" / "Beard Club" — pick one canonical form per doc).
- Number mismatch ($80M revenue in para 2, $85M in para 4 — explain or fix).
- Date mismatch (5-month guarantee → six-month program).

**Procedure:** Build a mental list of every named entity and number. Check consistency across the whole draft. Pick canonical forms; find-and-replace the variants.

### Scan 4 — Homophone scan

Standard error set:
- its / it's
- your / you're
- their / there / they're
- then / than
- affect / effect
- who's / whose
- to / too / two
- lose / loose
- principal / principle
- advise / advice
- accept / except
- complement / compliment
- discrete / discreet
- stationary / stationery
- peak / peek / pique

### Scan 5 — Double words & dropped articles

- Adjacent repeats: *the the, a a, and and, of of, is is, that that, to to.*
- Missing *a / an / the* before a concrete noun where natural speech would include it.
- "a" vs "an" agrees with the following sound, not the letter (*an FBI agent*, *a university*).

### Scan 6 — Quote, bracket & apostrophe pairs

- Every open quote has a matching close. Smart quotes match (no stray straight quotes in the same block).
- Every open paren, bracket, or brace has a matching close.
- Apostrophes placed correctly: *company's* (not *companys*); *the '90s* (not *the 90's*).
- Em-dashes for pause, en-dashes for ranges (*2024–2026*).

### Scan 7 — Internal contradiction

Read the draft twice. First pass: list every claim. Second pass: does any later claim contradict an earlier one?

**Bug:** Page 4 says "We're not proposing a rebrand." Page 11 says "Phase one rebuilds the brand identity system."
**Fix:** Reconcile. Either it's a rebrand and the letter acknowledges it, or phase one is explicitly not a rebrand.

**Also catches:**
- Claim "fixed retainer, no surprises" → variable media fee buried later.
- Claim one audience early → pivot to another late without bridging.
- Claim "every dollar traced to a lever" → line item with no lever named.

## Procedure

1. Draft.
2. Run scans 1-7 in order.
3. For any fail, fix before moving on.
4. Re-run any scan where a fix might have introduced a new bug.
5. When all 7 pass, continue to quality gates (`gates.md`).

## The 3 counts that matter most

For pitches and proposals, the 3 failures that ruin reader trust fastest:

1. **Count ≠ list.** "Four words" followed by three. Caught in half a second. Faith collapses.
2. **Name misspelled.** Client's name wrong. Reader stops reading.
3. **Internal contradiction.** Letter promises one thing, scope delivers another. Doc reads as committee work.

These three are non-negotiable. The other four scans are hygiene.
