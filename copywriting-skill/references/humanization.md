# Humanization — the anti-AI-tell protocol

The goal: every piece of copy passes a blind-read test. A marketing director should bet real money a human wrote it.

This file is the quality gate for voice. Grep-runnable. Binary.

## The 4 tiers of banned vocabulary

### Tier 1 — AI-tell verbs (strip on sight)
elevate, unlock, harness, leverage, empower, revolutionize, transform (as puffed-up synonym for "change"), delve, dive deep, streamline.

### Tier 2 — AI-tell adjectives (strip on sight)
seamless / seamlessly, robust, cutting-edge, bleeding-edge, next-generation, state-of-the-art, best-in-class, world-class, industry-leading, game-changing, transformative, holistic, comprehensive (as filler), innovative (as filler), dynamic (as filler), passionate (as self-claim).

### Tier 3 — AI-tell nouns (strip on sight)
synergy / synergies, ecosystem (as buzzword), stakeholders (as catch-all), paradigm, journey (as metaphor), solution (as filler — name the thing), bandwidth (as metaphor), SaaS (use "software" / "platform" / name the category), flywheel (use "engine" / "loop" / describe the mechanic).

### Tier 4 — Banned phrases (strip on sight)
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "In the modern era"
- "In an ever-evolving landscape"
- "Now more than ever"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Let's dive in"
- "Let's explore"
- "It's important to note that"
- "It goes without saying"
- "Needless to say"
- "At the intersection of X and Y"
- "More than just"
- "It's not just X, it's Y" (as default construction)
- "We believe"
- "Passionate about"
- "Deliver value"
- "Drive results"
- "Move the needle"
- "Actionable insights"
- "Thought leadership"
- "Mission-driven" (as self-claim)
- "Meaningful impact"

### Corporate hedges (always cut)
- "help to [verb]" → just [verb]
- "work to [verb]" → just [verb]
- "strive to [verb]" → just [verb]
- "seek to [verb]" → just [verb]
- "aim to [verb]" → just [verb]
- "committed to [verb-ing]" → just [verb]
- "dedicated to [verb-ing]" → just [verb]

## Punctuation discipline

### Em-dashes — strict quota
- Max 1 em-dash per 200 words.
- No em-dash chains. Never two em-dashes in the same paragraph as default rhythm.
- Prefer period, colon, or parentheses.
- AI fingerprint: mid-sentence em-dash sandwiches as default rhythm. Cut.

### Exclamation marks — near-ban
- Zero per proposal doc.
- Max 1 per 2,000 words elsewhere.
- Every exclamation, the reader trusts you less.

### Ellipsis — moderate
- Max 2 per 1,000 words.
- Fine for trailing thoughts. Never for drama.

### Quotes — always smart
Smart quotes only. Never straight quotes. Single quotes for nested only.

## Rhythm variance — the > 4 rule

Sentence-length standard deviation must exceed 4 words.

- AI writes flat: every sentence 14-16 words. Stdev ~1.
- Humans write jagged: 3, 18, 8, 14, 2, 21, 6. Stdev ~7.

Count words per sentence. If variance is low, the fix isn't word swap — it's surgical resentencing. Break long sentences. Merge short repeats.

## Contractions — turn them on

Default to contracted forms: don't, we're, it's, you're, can't, won't, I'm, that's, here's, what's.

Uncontracted forms earn their keep only for: rare emphasis ("Do. Not. Do. That."), deliberate formality in luxury/legal/heritage register.

## Pronoun discipline

### "You" dominates
The reader is the subject. "You already know the feeling." "You've tried three agencies." "You don't need another pitch."

### "We" enters only on commitments
"We" is load-bearing. Use when the brand is taking a stance or making a promise. "We ship Friday." "We take three clients a quarter."

### Banned in customer-facing copy
- "the user" — clinical, engineer-brained.
- "the customer" — stiff.
- "the individual" — bureaucratic.
- "one" as pronoun — wrong-register.
- Vague "they" — always name the group on first appearance.

## The 15 AI rhythm tells (structural)

1. **Tri-colon default.** Every sentence ends "A, B, and C." Ban as default. Vary to 2, 4, or single-item emphasis.
2. **The "not just X, but Y" pair.** Overused. Use rarely.
3. **The escalation triad.** "It's not X. It's Y. It's Z." Max one per document.
4. **The "imagine a world" open.** Banned.
5. **The reader-fake.** "You might be asking yourself..." Usually banned.
6. **The pseudo-profound aside.** "And that's the thing." Cut unless it genuinely lands.
7. **The value-stack adjective line.** "efficient, scalable, and cost-effective" — banned outright. Use specifics.
8. **The verb-noun buzzword compound.** "unlock value," "drive growth," "harness power" — all banned.
9. **The polysyndeton overkill.** "fast and easy and simple and powerful." Pick one.
10. **Rule-of-three overuse.** Three-item parallel lists more than twice per paragraph = AI slop.
11. **The mirror sentence.** "We don't just X. We Y." Sparingly. Overused.
12. **The "in essence" / "essentially" bailout.** Never.
13. **The "we believe" virtue-signal.** Banned as self-claim. Show belief through action.
14. **The "at the intersection of X and Y" frame.** Banned.
15. **The corporate hedge cluster.** "We strive to deliver meaningful impact." Every word dead.

## The read-aloud test

Before output:
1. Read the copy out loud.
2. Does it sound like someone you'd text?
3. Do you run out of breath anywhere?
4. Is there a single sentence you'd skip?
5. Would you say it at a bar?

Any "no" = rewrite.

## The strip-brand test

1. Swap the brand name for a direct competitor's.
2. Does the copy still make sense? Does it still fit?

If yes → the copy is generic. Rewrite with brand-specific specificity.
If no → the copy is owned. Ship.

## 6 before/after detoxes

### Example 1 — About us
BEFORE: We're passionate about leveraging cutting-edge solutions to help empower your brand to unlock its full potential.
AFTER: Your brand can sell more. We know how.

### Example 2 — Hero
BEFORE: Revolutionizing the way modern consumers approach grooming.
AFTER: Better shave. Fewer steps. No gimmicks.

### Example 3 — Pricing
BEFORE: Our comprehensive suite of innovative solutions delivers actionable insights that drive meaningful impact for your business.
AFTER: You get a 12-page teardown. Three weeks. $18,000.

### Example 4 — Case study
BEFORE: We partnered with a leading DTC brand to elevate their positioning in a highly competitive marketplace.
AFTER: A shave club was losing to Harry's. We rewrote their pitch in four weeks. Open rate doubled.

### Example 5 — Manifesto line
BEFORE: In a world where brands compete for attention, we believe meaningful connection is the ultimate differentiator.
AFTER: Most brands beg. We'd rather be ignored than beg.

### Example 6 — Close
BEFORE: We're incredibly excited to partner with you on this journey and look forward to delivering meaningful impact together.
AFTER: If this is the right read, say so. We'll send the next doc Monday.

## Final grep check

Before shipping, run this pattern against the output:

```
grep -iE "(elevate|unlock|harness|leverage|seamless|empower|robust|cutting-edge|world-class|best-in-class|game-changing|revolutionize|transformative|holistic|synergy|ecosystem|journey|delve|dive deep|SaaS|flywheel|in today's|passionate about|we believe|strive to|committed to|dedicated to|help to)"
```

Zero matches = ship. Any match = rewrite the line.
