# Frameworks — Attention cluster

The classics. Three frameworks for catching attention and routing it toward an action. Use when the reader's state is somewhere between "casually scrolling" and "actively comparing."

---

## AIDA — Attention / Interest / Desire / Action

The default landing-page and hero-section structure. Walks the reader from "what's this" to "I want it" to "I'll do something about it."

**Structure:**
- **A — Attention.** Hook line. Stops the scroll. Often a contrarian claim, surprising stat, or pointed question.
- **I — Interest.** Earn the read. Show specificity, name the reader's pain or curiosity, deliver one piece of real value.
- **D — Desire.** Show transformation. What does the reader's life look like after the product? Specific, concrete, image-driven.
- **A — Action.** One clear ask. Singular. Specific. With a low next step.

**Beat count:** 4. Roughly 1-1-2-1 word weighting (Action stays small).

**When to pick:** Landing pages, hero sections, ads with room for a setup. The default.

**Mini example:**
> A: Most landing pages lose 60% of readers in the first scroll.
> I: You've got 6 seconds before they bounce. Less if the headline doesn't earn it.
> D: Imagine a page that holds them. Specific number on it. Specific reader-pain on it. One offer, one ask. They scroll to it.
> A: Audit your top page. Free. 20 minutes.

---

## PAS — Problem / Agitate / Solve

Problem-led. The reader sees their own pain on the page before you offer the cure. Faster than AIDA. Higher conversion on cold lists.

**Structure:**
- **P — Problem.** Name the pain in the reader's own language. Specific, not generic.
- **A — Agitate.** Twist the knife. Add cost, time, embarrassment, missed opportunity. Make the pain feel present.
- **S — Solve.** Resolution. Your product, your method, your offer. One sentence ideally.

**Beat count:** 3. Often 1-2-1 word weighting (Agitate carries the bulk).

**When to pick:** Paid ads, cold-list emails, problem-aware audiences who haven't seen your category solution yet.

**Mini example:**
> P: You wrote the deck. Three different versions. None of them landed.
> A: Now the meeting's Friday. The team will read it on the call. Half of them will tune out by slide three. The other half will ask the question you can't answer.
> S: The 12-page teardown. Three days. We rewrite the deck cold, you read it Thursday night.

---

## BAB — Before / After / Bridge

Transformation-led. Shows the reader where they are, where they could be, and how you get them there. Stronger than PAS for solution-aware audiences who don't need pain explained.

**Structure:**
- **B — Before.** Where the reader is right now. Specific, observable.
- **A — After.** Where they'll be when this works. Same level of specificity.
- **B — Bridge.** The product, method, or offer that gets them across.

**Beat count:** 3. Roughly even weighting.

**When to pick:** Transformation pitches, course launches, B2B advisory, fitness/health/finance — anywhere the reader can imagine the after-state.

**Mini example:**
> Before: Your pitch deck reads like every other pitch deck.
> After: Your deck reads like a Harvard case study — investors lean in, take notes, ask sharper questions.
> Bridge: Three sessions with us. Two weeks. You leave with a deck no one's seen before.

## Cluster rules

1. **One framework per draft.** Don't braid AIDA into PAS in draft one.
2. **The structure shows up in the word count.** AIDA's "A" should be short; "I" and "D" carry length; final "A" is shortest.
3. **Specificity is the substance.** All three frameworks die when the words are generic.
4. **The framework dictates beat count, not voice.** You can write AIDA in Trott or Schwartz or Bernbach. The structure stays; the texture changes.

## When this cluster is wrong

- **Wrong for very short copy** (headline only, CTA only) — frameworks need room. Use a headline formula instead.
- **Wrong for awareness-state work** — Schwartz 5-stage handles cold audiences better. Pull `frameworks-awareness.md`.
- **Wrong for product detail** — FAB handles feature-level claims; AIDA is too high-level. Pull `frameworks-sales.md`.
