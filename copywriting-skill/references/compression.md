# Compression — ruthless word economy

Cut 40-60% from every hot draft. Every word earns its place. Respect the reader's time — every extra word is a small demand on their attention.

## The 5 compression principles

1. **Draft hot, edit cold.** Every first draft is 2x too long. Assume it. Cut on revision, not on the first pass.
2. **40% rule.** Every revision pass aims to cut 40%. Word-count the hot draft and the final. Difference < 40% = cut again.
3. **The skeleton test.** Strip every word but nouns and verbs. If the skeleton communicates, the rest was fat.
4. **The text-message test.** Would you text this to a friend? If no, rewrite.
5. **The strip-brand test.** Swap the brand name for a competitor's. If the copy still fits, it's generic — rewrite for specificity.

## Kill words (delete on sight)

### Adverbs (90% are dead weight)
very, really, quite, somewhat, rather, fairly, mostly, generally, basically, literally, actually, definitely, totally, completely, absolutely, incredibly, essentially, simply, clearly, obviously.

Most adverbs signal a weak verb. "Walked quickly" → "ran." "Acted decisively" → "acted." "Very good" → "great" or a specific stake.

### Filler phrases
- "Needless to say" → cut.
- "It goes without saying" → cut.
- "Obviously" → cut.
- "In my opinion" / "I think" → just say it.
- "As you can see" → cut.
- "Basically" / "Essentially" → cut.
- "Just" → cut 90% of the time.
- "At this juncture" → "now" or cut.
- "Moving forward" → "next" or cut.

### Weak openers
- "There is..." / "There are..." → rewrite with a real subject.
- "It is..." → rewrite.
- "The fact that..." → cut "the fact that," keep the rest.

### Latinate → Saxon

| Latinate | Saxon |
|----------|-------|
| utilize | use |

| terminate | end |

## The 6 cutting passes (run in order)

### Pass 1 — The nuclear cut
Delete every adverb, every "that," every filler phrase, every corporate hedge ("help to," "strive to," "committed to"). Don't replace yet. See what survives.

### Pass 2 — The verb upgrade
Replace Latinate with Saxon. Replace passive with active. Replace "to be" constructions with action verbs where possible.

### Pass 3 — The sentence split/merge
Any sentence over 25 words splits. Any sentence under 4 words considers merging (but short is a tool — often stays).

### Pass 4 — The paragraph prune
If a paragraph's first sentence can be cut and the rest still works, cut it. If the last sentence can be cut, cut it. Most first and last sentences of weak paragraphs are throat-clearing.

### Pass 5 — The read-aloud
Read out loud. Anywhere you stumble, re-cut.

### Pass 6 — The 40% check
Word-count. If you cut less than 40%, run passes 1-4 again.

## Sentence surgery — 5 named moves

1. **Nominalization reverse.** Nouns disguising verbs are fat. "Made a decision" → "decided." "Conducted an investigation" → "investigated." "Gave consideration to" → "considered."
2. **Active voice swap.** "Was written by Bryan" → "Bryan wrote." "Is being considered by the team" → "the team considers."
3. **Prepositional dump.** "A book about the history of X" → "a history of X." "A look at the future of X" → "the future of X."
4. **Clause pruning.** "A product which is designed to help users save time" → "a product that helps users save time" → "a product to help save time."
5. **Connective prune.** "And then" → ". Then." "And which" — cut the weakest half.

## 5 worked examples

### Example 1 — About-us
HOT (47 words):
> We are passionate about helping our clients unlock their full potential through our comprehensive suite of innovative marketing solutions that are designed to elevate their brand and drive meaningful impact in today's dynamic marketplace.

COLD (8 words):
> We make brands sell more. Want to talk?

### Example 2 — Hero
HOT (19 words): Introducing the most revolutionary approach to grooming that modern consumers have been waiting for.
COLD (5 words): A better shave. Fewer steps.

### Example 3 — Value prop
HOT (31 words): Our proprietary methodology combines strategic thinking with creative excellence to deliver holistic, integrated marketing solutions that drive measurable results for ambitious brands across every channel.
COLD (11 words): We write the pitch. You close the round. We overlap once.

### Example 4 — Founder bio
HOT (25 words): A seasoned industry veteran with extensive experience leveraging strategic insights to deliver transformative outcomes for best-in-class brands across diverse verticals.
COLD (11 words): Fourteen brands launched. Six I'd work with again. The rest taught me.

### Example 5 — Sign-off
HOT (16 words): We sincerely appreciate your time and look forward to the possibility of working together soon!
COLD (6 words): Happy to talk whenever you're ready.

## When NOT to compress

- **Don't sacrifice clarity for brevity.** If readers need context, give it.
- **Don't strip proof.** Specific numbers, named examples, dates — those must stay.
- **Don't kill rhythm.** Three short sentences in a row need one breath.
- **Don't remove the texture that makes it human.** Occasional "well," "look," "the thing is" lands when used sparingly.
- **Don't delete stakes.** "Book the call" is weaker than "Book before the Aug 1 cohort fills."

## Final discipline

When in doubt, cut another 20%. Then read it aloud. If it still works, the cut was right.
